The Emu wars was a series of short battles between Australians and emus during 1932.

This battle occurred after the economic downturn of the Great Depression where Australia, who heavily relied on its export industries as such no other nation would be buying .

This resulted in more farmers but due to how many were not able to make money, most left for city work and for those who stayed, an Emu problem arose.

Emus are large tacky birds that were able to destroy fences and consume the little wheat farmers were able to grow in Australian soil.

Around 20 thousand emus cost struggling farmers millions in terms of lost profits, so much so they manage to convince their government to eradicate these pests with military grade weapons.

The initial battle was said to be a success but the statistic showed that between the 20,000 emus only 1000 were killed whilst using up 10,000 rounds of ammunition.
